Cost Formula ▼

All-In Cost = Sending Fee + (FX Margin% × Amount) + Network Fee + (Settlement Days × Daily Time Cost)

Daily time cost = Amount × (10.5% / 365) = $0.14/day for $500, based on SBP policy rate of 10.5%.

Wise uses mid-market rate so FX margin = 0%. Their fee is $1.17 + 0.8% = ~$5.17 on $500. Source: Wise Pricing

Western Union: $0 fee on bank transfers >$200, but 2–4% FX margin. Source: WU Rate Analysis

Channel Specs ▼

Banks (SWIFT) — Highest send fees ($20–35) + SWIFT network charges ($15–25). FX margins 3.8–4.5%. Settlement 3–4 business days.

MTOs — Western Union ($0 fee + FX markup), Wise (mid-market + flat fee), Remitly ($1.99 + FX markup). Same-day delivery. Sources: Wise comparison

Mobile Wallets — JazzCash/Easypaisa via Remitly/WorldRemit. Minimal fees, tight spreads. Arrives in minutes. Source: WorldRemit

Stablecoin P2P — On-ramp fee (0.1%), USDT TRC-20 transfer ($1), P2P spread (~0.9%). Delivery ~2 hours. Carries unpriced counterparty risk. Sources: Binance, OKX

Limitations ▼
  • Stablecoin rates are volatile; snapshot valid at time of collection only.
  • P2P trades carry counterparty/settlement risk not priced in this analysis.
  • Bank and MTO fees assume standard retail. Corporate/volume rates are lower.
  • Exchange rate fixed at 279.60; actual rates fluctuate daily (Source: Forex.pk).
  • Regulatory compliance costs for stablecoins not included. Pakistan's VA Act 2026 passed but SBP operational guidelines pending.
  • Wise fee based on $1.17 + 0.8% structure (source). Remitly: $1.99 + variable FX (source).

Regulatory Context

What would SBP need to enable compliant stablecoin remittance corridors?

Current Legal Status

Pakistan's Virtual Assets Act 2026 established PVARA as an autonomous regulator. However, SBP has not issued specific authorization for stablecoin remittance corridors. The 2018 advisory against virtual assets was driven by absent legal framework — that framework now exists, but operational authorization has not followed.

FX Manual Constraints

The SBP Foreign Exchange Manual requires inward remittances through authorized dealers only. No provision for exchange platforms. Chapter 20 capital account restrictions don't recognize tokenized assets.

Licensing Pathway

  1. PVARA licensing for stablecoin service providers. The SECP sandbox approved real estate tokenization in its 2nd cohort (2025).
  2. SBP FX Manual amendment to create "virtual current account" instruments or designate licensed stablecoin platforms as authorized dealers.
  3. SECP-SBP coordination on custody and investor protection standards.

Bottom Line

The cost advantage is real (stablecoin ~1.3% vs banks ~13.3%), but partly reflects absent regulatory overhead. Until SBP issues operational authorization, stablecoin corridors remain in regulatory gray area. This analysis is informational, not a recommendation.
